Anish Nethi

I am a master’s student in Computer Science at the University of Texas at Austin, advised by Prof. Kristen Grauman. My work focuses on computer vision and multi‑modal perception, with an emphasis on integrating vision, language and audio to build robust perception systems. Prior to this I completed my B.Tech. in Computer Science and Engineering from Mahindra University, where I ranked 5th in my cohort with a CGPA of 8.84/10.0.

I have gained diverse research and industrial experience at Amazon Web Services, IIT Guwahati, Arizona State University, Mahindra University and the National University of Singapore. My coursework includes visual recognition, scientific computation for machine learning and generative models, and my long‑term goal is to build perception systems that reason about complex environments using multi‑modal cues.

Research collaborations: I am open to collaborating on research projects and mentoring students interested in computer vision and multi‑modal learning. Feel free to shoot me an email to discuss potential collaborations or internship opportunities.

Projects

Language‑Guided Reinforcement Learning for Smooth Multi‑View Video Transitions

Feb 2025 – May 2025

Designed a composite reward function balancing informativeness, transition smoothness and narrative alignment to optimise camera view selection in multi‑view ego–exo instructional videos. Applied PPO, A2C and DQN; PPO achieved the most balanced switching behaviour with a 10.6 % switch rate.

Predicting Naturalistic Eye Scanpaths from Saliency Maps

Jul 2023 – Jul 2024

Developed mathematical models that combine visual saliency maps with time‑coordinated data to simulate a person’s gaze on a scene. Achieved 83 % accuracy in simulating eye movements while reducing computational overhead.

Data Cleaning using Large Language Models

Sep 2024 – Dec 2024

Built a pipeline leveraging Intel’s Neural‑Chat LLM to detect and correct errors in structured datasets. Integrating metadata and refining prompt engineering yielded 89.6 % error‑correction accuracy.
